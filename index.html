About Me

Hello everyone!

I am a Chemical Engineer from Ecuador at Escuela Superior Politécnica del Litoral (ESPOL)
Devoted to materials science, drug discovery, and cancer research with formal coursework and experience
in computer science and molecular simulations. Since 2022, I have been part of summer programs, internships,
courses, and collaborations at organizations and universities such as Google Summer of Code, Toronto
University, ESPOL, University of California, Berkeley, Deep Forest Sciences (DFS), and Deepchem
(open-source GitHub repository). My work focuses on applying Deep Learning, Data Science, and AI algorithms
for Biology, Physics, and Chemistry. Currently, I am working as a researcher at Centro de Investigación y
Desarrollo en Nanotecnología (CIDNA), ESPOL, in machine-learning (ML) for materials science and molecular
simulations (molecular docking, molecular dynamics, etc) for cancer research. In addition, I am doing
research at DFS and DeepChem, leveraging equivariant models for drug discovery. Also, I have developed
personal projects using NLP and ML, such as building generative models and genetic algorithms.

Extracurricular activities are part of my life, too. For example, I am part of the TAWS programming club,
where we organize Hackathons, inclusive activities, and talks for students. I also enjoy Latin American
literature, especially Cortázar, Borges, and Rulfo. In addition, I love Latin rhythms like salsa, bossa nova,
and tango. These are part of my life since I started playing these tunes on my guitar about 10 years ago.
In addition, I like to learn from other cultures, so I became a language enthusiast, becoming proficient
in English and Spanish and intermediate in French and Portuguese.

There are nearly 30 000 molecules that we humans know about, let's
find out more of them!
Normalizing Flows is a generative model that performs non-linear
transformations between probability distributions. It starts with
a base case (i.e. Gaussian, Bernoulli, Pareto) and applies
transformations on the distribution based on a known molecule dataset
(QM9). This transformed distribution can take samples and find new
molecules.
Source code

Institution: Google Summer of Code, 2022 - DeepChem.
Language: Python.

Normalizing Flows

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is an key tool for interpretation
and is used in different fields such as chatbots, translators, and
science. BERT transformers are an important part of NLP, as they are
algorithms that learn from the context defined by a user. ESPOL Q&A
Website is a question and answer program where the user can ask
questions about ESPOL University and get the answers through the use
of BERT transformers. In order to be able to respond to the user,
ESPOL Q&A Website gathers data using web scrapping, from official and
reviewed sites, each time a question is being asked. Overall, this
project proves the various uses of BERT transformers, from Q&A
websites to life sciences.
Source code

Institution: ESPOL University, TAWS Programming Club, 2022.
Language: Python, HTML, MySQL.

ESPOL Q&A Website

In Ecuador, agro-industries were built under empirical knowledge but
there is a growing opportunity for technology. Data Science and AI are
powerful tools that can be used to computerized methods and optimize
processes.
Essential Oils optimization is a multivariate optimization that maximizes
yield while minimizing costs. The main structure of this work is to get
the inputs and outputs of the essential oils industry as vectors and
perform a regression on them. Once the regression presents a considerable
result, based on MSE and R^2, the multivariate optimization algorithm
determines the conditions for the facility to work.
Source

Institution: ESPOL and University of Toronto Collaboration, 2021.
Language: Python.
